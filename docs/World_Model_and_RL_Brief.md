# TropCool: Transformer World Model for Tropical Data-Center Cooling

## What I built

**TropCool** is a **transformer-based world model** that forecasts next-hour data-center (DC) cooling outcomes from a **24-hour context** of weather, plant configuration, operating setpoints, and IT load. The model is trained on **synthetic trajectories** generated by coupling a **physics-style DC cooling simulator** to **real hourly weather archives**—approximately **twenty years** of Open-Meteo history (2004–2024) for **six Southeast Asian cities**: Cyberjaya, Singapore, Jakarta, Bangkok, Johor Bahru, and Ho Chi Minh City. Each timestep carries tropical-relevant drivers (dry-bulb temperature, humidity, wet bulb, time-of-day, tariff, and facility parameters) and predicts **rack inlet temperature**, **cooling power**, **tower water use**, **PUE**, and **operating cost**.

The world model is **option-2 conditioned**: the input includes not only the past 24 hours but also **planned next-hour controls and IT/electricity context**, matching how operators commit setpoints before observing the outcome. This makes the mapping from actions to next-hour targets **learnable** even when controls and loads are not perfectly autocorrelated.

**Calibration to real DC data:** I **fine-tuned** the pretrained world model on **public Kaggle-style operational CSVs** (building-scale HVAC and cold-aisle telemetry), with **column mapping, unit conversion (e.g., °F→°C, RT→MW), and hourly alignment**. Targets for that stage were kept **consistent with the simulator’s label space** so normalization and heads remain compatible with the main checkpoint. Together, **synthetic diversity at scale** plus **real-data adaptation** is meant to reduce sim-to-real gap without abandoning the structured target definitions (PUE, cost, water) used in training.

**Downstream use:** the same forward model powers a **Gymnasium environment** (`WorldModelEnv`) for reinforcement learning: each step applies **(chiller setpoint, CRAH fan speed, tower fan speed)**, rolls the transformer one hour forward on **real weather for a chosen city**, and returns reward as **negative predicted cost**, with a large penalty if predicted **rack inlet temperature exceeds 27 °C**. Stable-Baselines3 **SAC** trains against this environment—**orders of magnitude faster per step** than stepping the full physics simulator.

## Key results (reporting line)

| Metric | Value / status |
|--------|----------------|
| **World model — PUE (held-out evaluation)** | On the primary **synthetic episode validation** setup (aligned features, MinMax targets, weighted loss), the model achieves **PUE R² ≈ 0.91–0.93** (typical run **~0.92**). Exact figures depend on checkpoint, parquet split, and PUE loss weighting; re-run `evaluate_world_model.py` after each training refresh for the authoritative number. |
| **RL vs baseline — predicted cooling cost** | **Singapore A+B pilot (May 2026):** `train_sac_tropcool.py` with **512 h** episodes, **`--action-box baseline`**, **`--eval-aligned-fraction 0.4`**, **500k** steps, **no reward norm** → **~7%** mean savings on **27 scenarios** (single shared SAC checkpoint evaluated per city in pilot log). **Per-city v2 assembly (`data/rl/per_city_v2/`):** A+B retrain for **cyberjaya, jakarta, ho_chi_minh_city** + **singapore** pilot; **bangkok / johor_bahru** kept from legacy **`data/rl/per_city/`** (1M-step). **Strict gate** (`compare_policies.py --mean-over-scenarios --episode-len 512 --require-sac-beats-baseline-all`): **PASS 5/6 cities**; **FAIL jakarta** (SAC mean **1,210,732 RM** vs baseline **1,163,253 RM**). **Mean Y across 6 cities ≈ 6.1%** when including Jakarta. **Rack violations:** **0** on all scenarios. Log: **`data/rl/per_city_v2_strict_eval.log`**. **Simulator spot-check (Singapore, 2019, 720 h):** SAC wins on **world model** but **not** on **`dc_simulator`** ordering — see **`data/rl/simulator_spotcheck.log`**. |

**Jakarta (honest portfolio boundary):** Per-city SAC under the frozen `compare_policies.py` contract can **regress vs baseline** on some sites even when the five other cities pass. **Jakarta** is kept in the dashboard (map, climate grid, 24 h traces) but **excluded from the validated SAC portfolio**: strict `--require-sac-beats-baseline-all` and headline **mean Y** for deployment claims use only **Cyberjaya, Singapore, Bangkok, Johor Bahru, and Ho Chi Minh City** (`--exclude-cities jakarta`). Jakarta is labeled **baseline recommended** in the UI when SAC mean cost is not strictly below baseline on that contract.

**Climate headline metrics (X / Y + savings at weather):** For each city and warming horizon (2030 / 2040 / 2050), roll the **world model** on **synthetic projection weather** (`scripts/generate_climate_weather.py`) with **baseline** vs **per-city SAC** (`data/rl/per_city_v2/`, `--action-box baseline`, calendar year **2019**, `weather_start_row` **0**). Using **annualized `cost_RM`**: **X** = 100 × (baseline_future − baseline_current) / baseline_current; **Y** = 100 × (SAC_future − SAC_current) / SAC_current (current = 2025 / `current` period). **Mitigation** = X − Y (percentage points of *cost growth* avoided). **SAC savings at that weather** = 100 × (1 − SAC_future / baseline_future) on the **same** future CSV—an easy pitch (“at 2040 weather, SAC cuts cost by Z%”). **Central vs stress:** IPCC-style central deltas are **+0.5 / +1.2 / +2.0°C** (2030–2050); optional **stress** periods **`2040_stress` (+2.5°C)** and **`2050_stress` (+3.0°C)** use the same humidity adjustment as central. **`validated_portfolio_mean`** averages the five cities that pass the strict SAC gate (**excludes Jakarta**). Outputs: `dashboard/climate_results.json` → `headlines_v2`; regenerate with `generate_climate_weather.py` then `run_climate_extended_results.py` (default **8760 h** requested, clamped to **8736 h** for calendar year 2019; `--fast` → 720 h).

**How to read “Y %” once training completes:** pick a single evaluation contract (e.g., calendar year **2019**, **720 h**, same seed), compute **mean total predicted cost** over six cities for baseline vs SAC, then **Y = 100 × (1 − cost_SAC / cost_baseline)**. For **lower-variance** scores over multiple weather slices, use `compare_policies.py --mean-over-scenarios` (defaults: **years 2018–2020**, **start_rows 0 / 2160 / 4320**, **seeds 42–44**, Cartesian product → **27 scenarios** per city); the script prints **per-city mean ± std cost_RM** and **Y** from the mean of per-city means. For **maximum coverage** of the shared archive (baseline and other policies use the **same** scenario list), use **`--eval-wide-coverage`**: intersection of calendar years across all six CSVs, evenly spaced **start_row** per year capped by the **shortest** city-year window, tunable via **`--eval-coverage-starts-per-year`** and **`--eval-seeds`** (prefer a single seed if scenario count is large). The world model is the **surrogate** for cost here—final operations should still be validated against meters and safety limits.
